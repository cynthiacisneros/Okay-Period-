What Is a Period?

A simple guide to what’s happening in your body.

A period (menstruation) is monthly bleeding from the uterus through the vagina. It’s part of your menstrual cycle—your body’s rhythm of preparing for a possible pregnancy, then resetting if pregnancy does not occur.

Three things to know

  • 🩸

    The lining sheds

    Your uterus builds a lining each month. If pregnancy does not happen, your body sheds that lining.

  • 📅

    It happens monthly

    Periods usually happen about once a month, but everyone’s cycle can be a little different.

  • 🌸

    It’s normal

    Periods are a normal part of growing up, even if they feel confusing at first.

What happens in a cycle?

  1. ✨

    Your body prepares

    The uterus builds up a lining.

  2. 💫

    Hormones shift

    Hormones help guide changes in the cycle.

  3. 💗

    Your period begins

    The lining leaves the body through the vagina.

  4. 🔁

    The cycle starts again

    The body resets and begins preparing again.

Why it matters 💡

Learning about periods can help you:

  • Feel less confused
  • Know what is normal for your body
  • Feel more prepared
  • Know when to ask for help

Everyone’s body is different. If something worries you, talking to a trusted adult or healthcare provider is always okay.
