What Is a Period?

A period (menstruation) is monthly bleeding from the uterus through the vagina. It is one part of the menstrual cycle, the body’s rhythm of preparing for a possible pregnancy and then resetting if pregnancy does not occur.

What happens in a typical cycle?

The lining of the uterus thickens. If no fertilized egg implants, hormone levels shift, the lining sheds, and blood and tissue leave the body—often over several days. Cycle length and flow vary widely from person to person and can change over time.

Why it matters

Understanding periods helps people recognize what is normal for them, plan for comfort and hygiene, and know when to ask a healthcare professional for guidance.
